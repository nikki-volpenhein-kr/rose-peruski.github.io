A11y Snack Talks

Problem Statement

Agreement that Accessibility and inclusivity are important and we do not have good products unless they are accessible. Knowledge level on accessibility is low or non-existent. Training takes time and energy. Office Hours also needed a boost to give people the knowledge to know what questions to ask.

Past Trainings

  • General Accessibility Training - 1 hour
  • HTML Formatted Training - 1 hour

Introducing Snack Talks

  • According to an article from the Alliance for Academic Internal Medicine, doctors Avraham Z. Cooper, MD and Jeremy B. Richards, MD, MA stated, “adult learning data suggest that typical learner attention span wanes after about 15 to 20 minutes.”
  • The snack talk accomplishes multiple things
    • Keeps attendees engaged
    • Maximizes learning
    • Allows time for office hours
    • Smaller time ask for live viewers and those watching the recording
  • "Snack Talk": A bite-sized piece of information that takes as long to consume as your afternoon snack

The Snack Talk Format

  • Stay on a single topic:
    • Stays within time constraints
    • Can go into appropriate depth without losing participants
  • Go back to the source: we often use HTML pages to explain the connection between what we see and what AT understands
